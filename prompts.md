# Gemini Prompt Engineering: Before & After Rewrites  This file documents 5 prompt rewrites tested in Gemini, demonstrating how specific constraints, context, and formatting instructions fundamentally change the quality and structure of the AI's output.  ---  ## 1. Code Refactoring & Optimization  ### Before (Weak) > How do I make my Python loop faster?  ### After (Strong) > Act as a Senior Python Developer. I have a script that iterates through a list of 100,000 dictionaries to calculate a metric, and it is running slowly. Give me 3 distinct optimization strategies (e.g., list comprehensions, vectorization with pandas, or multiprocessing). For each strategy, provide a brief code snippet and a 1-sentence explanation of why it improves execution speed.  ### Output Difference * **With the Weak Prompt:** Gemini typically gives a generic, textbook response listing random Python speed tips (like "use local variables" or "avoid global scopes") without practical, scalable code. * **With the Strong Prompt:** Gemini targets the exact data structure (dictionaries). It provides a structured breakdown with side-by-side, copy-pasteable code examples comparing a slow `for-loop` against a fast `pandas` approach, specifically tailored to large datasets.  ---  ## 2. Professional Communication & Tone Control  ### Before (Weak) > Tell my team we missed the deadline.  ### After (Strong) > Write an internal Slack announcement to a remote software development team. Announce that we missed the shipping deadline for Version 2.0 due to a critical bug found during QA. The tone must be accountable, encouraging, and calm—not blaming. Include a clear, bulleted list of the next 3 steps we are taking today, and close by thanking them for their hard work. Keep it under 150 words.  ### Output Difference * **With the Weak Prompt:** Gemini often generates a robotic, overly formal memo that sounds cold or corporate, missing the human element needed for morale during a setback. * **With the Strong Prompt:** Gemini outputs a perfectly formatted Slack message using emojis contextually. It strikes the exact balance of taking ownership while maintaining team psychological safety, delivering clear action items immediately.  ---  ## 3. Data Analysis & Framework-Driven Synthesis  ### Before (Weak) > Give me marketing strategies for a coffee shop.  ### After (Strong) > Act as a growth marketing consultant. I am launching a specialty, organic coffee shop in a dense urban neighborhood targeting Gen Z college students. Use the SWOT analysis framework to evaluate our position, and then provide 3 highly specific, low-budget gorilla marketing ideas tailored to this demographic. Present the SWOT analysis as a markdown table.  ### Output Difference * **With the Weak Prompt:** Gemini spits out cliché, obvious advice like "post on social media" or "have a grand opening," which applies to literally any business anywhere. * **With the Strong Prompt:** Gemini forces itself into a structured table (SWOT) and restricts its ideas to fit a tiny budget and a very specific target audience (Gen Z students). The resulting ideas focus on things like TikTok trends, student discounts, and eco-friendly packaging initiatives.  ---  ## 4. Complex Concept Simplification (ELIF)  ### Before (Weak) > Explain APIs.  ### After (Strong) > Explain the concept of an API (Application Programming Interface) to a non-technical marketing manager. Use the specific analogy of a restaurant waiter, customer, and kitchen to explain how data moves. Avoid all coding jargon, and keep the explanation under 200 words.  ### Output Difference * **With the Weak Prompt:** Gemini gives a technical definition, explains REST vs. SOAP, talks about JSON/XML payloads, and overwhelms a non-technical person with terminology. * **With the Strong Prompt:** Gemini restricts its vocabulary. It maps the components perfectly: the Customer is the user, the Waiter is the API delivering the request, and the Kitchen is the server database. The output is brief, vivid, and immediately understandable.  ---  ## 5. Structured Content Creation  ### Before (Weak) > Write a newsletter about productivity.  ### After (Strong) > Write a weekly email newsletter on personal productivity. Structure the newsletter into three distinct sections: > 1. "The Big Thought": A 3-sentence breakdown of why "time blocking" beats standard to-do lists. > 2. "Action Item": A step-by-step micro-habit the reader can implement in under 2 minutes today. > 3. "Quote of the Week": A relevant, motivating quote from a historical figure. > Maintain an conversational, engaging, and brief tone.  ### Output Difference * **With the Weak Prompt:** Gemini generates a massive wall of text—essentially a generic blog post masquerading as an email, which suffers from low scannability. * **With the Strong Prompt:** Gemini acts as a precise editor. It delivers a highly scannable, beautifully spaced newsletter that honors the exact structural constraints, making it ready to drop straight into an email marketing platform.
