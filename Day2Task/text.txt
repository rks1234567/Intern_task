I asked ChatGPT, Mistral Le Chat, Gemini, and Claude.ai the same question:

“What are Large Language Models? Tokens, Training, Prediction”

All four models gave similar explanations, but each explained the concepts in a different style and structure.

1. ChatGPT

ChatGPT first explained what an LLM is.
Then it explained Tokens — how text is broken down into smaller parts — along with examples, why tokens matter, and some rough rules about token counts.

After that, it explained:

Training
Neural networks and parameters
Prediction
Why LLMs appear intelligent

Finally, it explained:

Fine-tuning
Alignment
2. Mistral Le Chat

Mistral explained all the concepts with more examples and focused only on the specific subtopics I asked about.
Its answers were more direct and concise, without adding extra topics.

3. Gemini

Gemini explained the concepts in a brief and structured way.

For Tokens, it explained:

What tokens are
Why tokenization is needed
How models process numbers and text differently

For Training, it divided the explanation into two phases:

Pretraining
Fine-tuning

Then, under Fine-tuning and Alignment, it further explained:

Instruction tuning
RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback)

For Prediction, it explained how models generate text step by step.

At the end, it also provided a short summary of all concepts.

4. Claude.ai

Claude gave an explanation similar to Gemini’s structure.
In addition, it explained:

Model size
Parameters
Rough analogies for different model sizes

For example:

Small models
~1B parameter models
Pattern recognition capabilities
Overall Observation

Although all the models explained the same core concepts — Tokens, Training, and Prediction — each model presented the information differently in terms of:

Structure
Depth
Examples
Additional concepts
Level of detail
